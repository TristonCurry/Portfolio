MICHEAL SWANGO

By: Triston Curry

Few names in medical history strike fear quite like that of Dr. Micheal Swango. A former physician turned serial killer, Swango’s chilling crimes spanned decades, leaving a trail of mysterious patient deaths across the United States, Africa, and beyond. This is a story of deception, malpractice, and murder, all hidden behind a doctor's white coat.

EARLY LIFE

Micheal was a new medical student at Southern Illinois University and also worked as an ambulance assistant on the side. Swango was no normal student, as mysterious events started to surround him.

FAKING CHECKUPS

Swango was caught faking OBGYN checkups and reports, often making up critical information regarding his patients. His patients rarely got better, and in some cases, they seemed to get worse or even pass away under his care.

SECOND CHANCE

The SIU Board decided to give Swango another chance as long as he repeated another year of OB/GYN rotations, which he completed and took an Internship at Ohio State University Medical Center in 1983.

TENSION AT OHIO STATE

As time went by at Ohio State for Swango, people reported finding him injecting unknown substances into patients. Many cases these patients would soon fall ill. An investigation was launched, where no wrongdoing was found. Swango was not re-hired after finishing his internship.

RETURN TO QUINCY

Swango returns to his Ambulance technician job in his hometown of Quincy, Illinois.

Co-Workers of Swango noticed when he would prepare the office coffee, they would feel ill. This was reported to authorities, the investigation found that he was putting arsenic into the coffee.

In August of 1985, Swango was charged with 6 counts of aggravated battery, resulting in 5 years of prison.

FIVE YEARS LATER...

Upon Swango’s release, he was determined to continue working in the medical field despite his troubling past. In an effort to hide his identity and make a fresh start, he legally changed his name to Daniel J. Adams. This new identity allowed him to apply for positions without raising immediate suspicion, giving him another opportunity to reenter the healthcare system unnoticed.

TRAVEL AGENDA

After his release, Swango moved from place to place, leaving behind a pattern of suspicious deaths and misconduct:

  • Newport News, Virginia (1989–1991): Lab technician; coworkers fell mysteriously ill. Girlfriend Kristin Kinney died by suspicious suicide.
  • Sioux Falls, South Dakota (1992): Faked credentials to gain medical residency; dismissed when past crimes uncovered.
  • Stony Brook, New York (1993): Psychiatric resident at VA hospital; fired after criminal record revealed.
  • Zimbabwe (1994–1995): Hospital patients died under his care; suspected poisoning incident involving landlord.
  • Zambia and Namibia (1995–1997): Continued medical work under unclear circumstances.
  • Saudi Arabia (1997): Hired at Dhahran hospital; Needs to travel back to U.S. to obtain travel visa.

THE ARREST OF MICHAEL SWANGO

In 1997, while attempting to secure a visa to continue practicing abroad, Swango returned to the United States. Federal authorities, who had been building a case against him for fraud and suspected murders, arrested him at Chicago’s O’Hare International Airport. Following a detailed investigation, Swango pleaded guilty to charges of fraud and later to the murder of multiple patients. He was sentenced to life in prison without the possibility of parole, bringing an end to his decades-long trail of deception, malpractice, and death.
